Artist Statement

At the intersection of computer science and painting, my works finds a rooted commonality in themes of data, structure and form.

My work attempts to create a dialogue to dissect and map algorithms through human experience, and human experience through algorithms (vaguely, what Bernard Chazelle describes as "natural algorithms.") Through a process of layering diagrams, maps, observational drawings, notes, and annotations, my works become a tool for self-interrogation and self-reflection, a way to foster a dialogue between my own left and right brain and the oft-siloed hemispheres of "tech" and "art."

I am interested in algorithmic transparency, ethics in relation to technology, and the nature of power inherit in what is the action of coding - to hide or conceal or transform a piece of data, to make new from layers of old.
